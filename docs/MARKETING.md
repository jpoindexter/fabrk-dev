# Fabrk Launch Marketing Guide

This document outlines the marketing strategy, messaging, and launch plan for Fabrk - The Anti-Bloat Next.js Boilerplate.

---

## Positioning Statement

**Fabrk is the essential-only Next.js SaaS boilerplate for indie hackers and solo developers who want TypeScript + PostgreSQL without the bloat of 1000-file competitors.**

### Target Audience

**Primary:**
- Indie hackers building their first SaaS
- Solo developers who want to ship fast
- First-time founders with technical background
- Developers tired of complex boilerplates

**Secondary:**
- Small development agencies (2-5 people)
- Technical co-founders in early-stage startups
- Developers building side projects
- Freelancers creating client SaaS products

**Psychographics:**
- Values simplicity over features
- Prefers understanding code over magic abstractions
- Wants modern stack (TypeScript, PostgreSQL, latest Next.js)
- Budget-conscious ($79 vs $199-349)
- Willing to add features as needed vs having everything pre-built

## Crisis Management

### Handling Negative Feedback

**Common Objections:**

1. **"This is just a template, not worth $79"**
   - Response: "Fair point! It's 31-46 hours of dev work, production-ready, maintained, and supported. If you value your time at $10/hour, it pays for itself. But I understand if you prefer to build from scratch - that's a valid choice too."

2. **"ShipFast has 1486 customers, why choose Fabrk?"**
   - Response: "ShipFast is great! But it's JavaScript-only, MongoDB-only, and $199. Fabrk is for developers who want TypeScript + PostgreSQL at a lower price. Different tools for different needs."

3. **"Only 80 components? Supastarter has 100+"**
   - Response: "That's the point - Fabrk is anti-bloat. 80+ essential components you'll actually use, not 100+ where 30 sit unused. You can always add more as you need them."

4. **"Why should I pay when there are free alternatives?"**
   - Response: "Free templates exist, but they're often incomplete, unmaintained, or outdated. Fabrk saves 31-46 hours, includes support, and gets lifetime updates. If your time is worth anything, it pays for itself."

### When to Refund (No Drama)

- Customer genuinely unhappy: Immediate refund
- Technical issue we can't fix: Refund + apology
- Not a good fit: Refund + recommend alternative
- Goal: <5% refund rate, 0% drama
